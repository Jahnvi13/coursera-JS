Aaloo Bhindi
Aaloo bhindi is a favourite in Indian households. It consists of Aaloo (potatoes) and Bhindi (Ladyfinger fancily called Okra) fried in oil for 10 minutes. It saves the day when mums are too confused what to pack for their kid's lunches.
Rajma-chawal
Rajma-chawal is the staple diet of North-India. Composed of Rajma (kidney-beans) cooked in a gravy with mystrious elements poured over chawal (rice), this Indian delicacy is sure to seduce you the moment you meet eyes with it.
Palak-paneer
Paneer (cottage cheese) is the vegetarian version of Chicken, Meat and Fish. It's all that vegetarians have got in the name of protien. A true palak-paneer lover licks the bowl so clean that nobody finds out what it had in the first place.
